PEAF 2026 is a **full-day workshop**. The detailed schedule with accepted papers and exact timings will be published after the review process.

## Schedule Overview

### Morning

| Session | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
|---|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Welcome and Introduction** | Introduction to the workshop, including defining shared terms, an overview of current work, and emerging challenges of evaluating the pedagogical quality of automated feedback.                                     |
| **Interactive Session** | Attendees evaluate real-world examples of automated feedback from various disciplines in small groups (K-12 English and mathematics, higher education mathematics, mechanical engineering, and other STEM subjects). |
| **Lightning Talks** | Short 5-minute presentations from participants on their submitted work |                                                                                                                                              |
| **Discussion of Presentations** | Attendees rotate around presenter tables for in-depth discussion of each submitted work.                                                                                                                             |
| **Morning Summary** | Organisers summarise key elements of the lightning talks, discussion, and interactive sessions.                                                                                                                      |

### Afternoon

| Session | Description |
|---|---|
| **Afternoon Opening** | Introduction to the afternoon's objectives and structure. Organisers and attendees (via MentiMeter) provide a list of topics as potential research avenues. |
| **Structured Research Design** | Attendees split into groups by topic of interest for structured discussion: related theory and literature, formulating research questions, methodology and data. Each stage is followed by group feedback to the wider workshop. |
| **Research Design Report and Collaboration Discussion** | Groups present their research ideas to the broader community, followed by open discussion and exploration of potential collaborations. |
| **Closing** | Organisers close the workshop with a summary of discussions throughout the day. |
